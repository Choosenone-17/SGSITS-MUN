Committee Description

The Social, Humanitarian, and Cultural Committee (SOCHUM) is a principal organ of the United Nations that addresses a wide range of global issues related to human rights, social development, humanitarian affairs, and cultural cooperation. SOCHUM plays a crucial role in promoting international peace and security by focusing on the well-being of individuals and communities worldwide. The committee deliberates on matters such as refugee crises, human trafficking, drug control, and the promotion of fundamental freedoms. It also works to enhance cooperation among member states in addressing social challenges and fostering cultural understanding. SOCHUM's efforts contribute significantly to the UN's mission of creating a more just and equitable world for all.
