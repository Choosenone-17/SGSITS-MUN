Committee Description

The Disarmament and International Security Committee (DISEC), also known as the First Committee of the United Nations General Assembly, is primarily responsible for addressing issues related to disarmament, global security, and the regulation of armaments. DISEC focuses on promoting international peace and security by discussing matters such as nuclear disarmament, arms control, non-proliferation of weapons of mass destruction (WMDs), and the prevention of armed conflicts. The committee plays a crucial role in fostering dialogue among member states, encouraging cooperation on security-related issues, and developing frameworks for reducing the threat of armed violence worldwide.
